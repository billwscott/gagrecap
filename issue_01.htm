EDITION # 1 OCTOBER, 2013

 

Roy Delgado reminded me Earl Temple was the first publisher of the Gag Recap.

However, my first memory of the Gag Recap was receiving a bright yellow booklet every month in the mail from Hedden Place in New Providence, New Jersey from Al Gottlieb. It was filled with cartoons from magazines that now no longer exist or don’t use cartoons now. There were so many places that published cartoons, you could actually buy a smaller version of Gag Recap that listed cartoons from technical journals.

 

The Gag Recap had descriptions of cartoons from great cartoonists like Bill Maul, George Dole, Bo Brown, Lo Linkert and many, many more.

 

The Recap would later start arriving from Chalfont, PA every month after Al sold the Recap to Bill Keough. You can still read about this in Bill’s Book ‘What Are You Nuts’ check it out here: http://www.yourbook.com/BookInfo/IP24005-07.asp).

 

Bill’s reign of the Gag Recap was highlighted by his ‘What Are You Nuts’ column, Speedy’s ‘From Gags to Riches’ column, the great cartoons that graced the cover of the Gag Recap and of course the other worldly letters from George Gibson at Broken Arrow, Oklahoma. Bill published it faithfully until he passed away December 17th, 2010. The Gag Recap has been absent from the scene since then.

 

So, I’d like to pay tribute to the previous publishers, Earl Temple, Al Gottlieb and Bill Keough.

Thanks to everyone for the encouragement to help revive this version of the Gag Recap.

The New Gag Recap’s mission is to provide cartoonists/writers information on published magazine and website cartoons. We also will report on existing markets and seek out new ones for freelance cartoonists. We additionally are hoping to help writers with markets as well.

  

CALLING ALL RECAPPERS

If anyone has access to other magazines with cartoons and can send me the recaps (email is fine), it would be appreciated. Also, let me know if there is a particular magazine that you want re-capped and I’ll do my best to include it in future issues. Thanks to Paul Kale’s efforts and David Prosten from UCS, we should be able to start recapping their cartoons in future issues.

There was no reason given, but I would have to believe it was due to their own economics. On 2/25/12’ I received a letter from them acknowledging publication of one of my cartoons. In it, the editor wrote, “We routinely hear how much our readers love the Funny Bone section. That success is attributable to you, the artist. Assuming that was accurate, I wonder what the reason could be (aside from plain cost cutting) they are not using cartoons anymore. Moreover, I wonder if a query from a newsletter editor like yourself (formatted in a checkbox yes or no way to making a reply easy) could lead to a feature on this issue in your pub. and perhaps open magazines to accepting cartoons again.”
